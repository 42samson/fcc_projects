Jimi Hendrix

The iconic and revolutionary guitar player
Jimi Hendrix, playing his signature upside down Fender Stratocaster on stage in 1967.

    The Life of a Legend:

  • 1942 - Born in Seattle, Washington.
  • 1957 - Began to play guitar at the age of 15.
  • 1961 - Enlisted in the US Army and was assigned to the 101st Airborne Division.
  • 1962 - Granted an honorable discharge from the Army after injuring himself in a paratrooper excersize.
  • 1966 - Moves to England after playing with the likes of Little Richard and the Isley Brothers.
  • 1967 - Achieves fame in the US after setting his guitar ablaze at the Monterrey Pop Festival.
  • 1968 - The Jimi Hendrix Experience records their third studio album, Electric Ladylyand.
  • 1969 - Hendrix headlines the Woodstok festival and performs a unique rendition of the Star Spangled Banner along with other hits.
  • August 1970 - Hendrix headlines the Isle of Wight festival
  • September 1970 - Hendrix is found, unresponsive, and dies of barbiturate-related asphyxia.
